Self-Improvement: Gym Routine

Status: Active

Description:

I'm actively trying to be better than I was yesterday. For that reason I've taken up a gym routine with the end goal of losing weight and building some muscle.

Since Feb 2017, I've been attempting to lose weight, and I systematically experimented with different foods/meal plans to get to the best possible results for me. Those results continue, since March 2017 to be about 2 lbs weight loss per week without any added activity.

As of Jan 31, 2018, I've been going to the gym on a MWF schedule.

Routine

My routine consists of the following:

  • Chest Press
  • Leg Press
  • Shoulder Press
  • Rowing
  • Swimming Laps

Rowing

To be completely honest, I've got no clue what I'm doing here -- I just know how to row and use it as a warmup. Currently, my goal has been to increase the total meters rowed by 100 every time, except I've now hit my limit, and I'm going to need to do it a bit more to improve.

Chart

Swimming

Much like Rowing, I don't really know how to structure these, so I started off gunning it and doing the best I can. I quickly found out, however, that after doing all of the rest of the exercises, my body gave out quickly with this method. So, I started putting breaks in between each lap. This took too long and didn't improve anything, so now I do two laps of the 25m pool and then a one minute break, followed by 2 more laps until my arms are too sore to continue. I'll keep doing this until things improve or I find a better way.
